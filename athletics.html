Tournament Tiers Explained

  • Platinum — JCT (Junior Championship Tournament)

    The most prestigious junior circuit events. Nationally sanctioned with the highest level of competition and the greatest ranking point weight.

  • Gold

    High-level regional tournaments with large competitive draws. Second tier of the junior circuit, drawing top juniors from across the country.

  • Silver

    Mid-tier junior tournaments with strong regional competition. Great stepping-stone events on the path to Gold level play.

  • Bronze

    Entry-level competitive junior tournaments, ideal for developing players building early tournament experience and ranking points.

How Squash Ratings Work

US Squash uses the Universal Squash Rating (USR) — a dynamic Elo-style number that updates weekly based on match results in sanctioned events. Winning against a higher-rated opponent gains more points; losing to a lower-rated opponent costs more. Ratings range roughly from 0 to 7+.

  • 0.0 – 2.0  ·  Beginner

    New to the sport. Still developing basic strokes and court movement.

  • 2.0 – 3.5  ·  Novice – Intermediate

    Consistent rallying with improving shot selection. Active in local club play.

  • 3.5 – 4.5  ·  Competitive Recreational

    Regular tournament competitors. Strong fundamentals and court awareness.

  • 4.5 – 5.5  ·  Advanced

    High-level junior players and serious adult competitors. Consistent in regional events.

  • 5.5 – 6.5  ·  Elite

    Top juniors nationally, collegiate varsity players, and strong adult professionals.

  • 6.5+  ·  World-Class

    PSA-ranked professionals and top collegiate players competing at the highest level.
