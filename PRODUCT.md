# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People keeping up with Nikhil: recruiters, VCs, potential cofounders, consultants, family, and collaborators. They arrive with some prior context (a referral, a LinkedIn profile, a conversation) and are looking to form a complete picture of who he is. They browse on desktop and mobile, often quickly — a recruiter scanning before a call, a founder deciding whether to reach out.

## Product Purpose

A life portfolio, not a resume site. The site exists to communicate Nikhil as a whole person — engineer, entrepreneur, athlete, videographer — and to convey his journey, not just his credentials. Success means a visitor finishes their session feeling like they genuinely understand him: what drives him, what he has built, what kind of person he is. The primary call to action is understanding, not conversion. Outreach follows naturally from that.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, clean, intriguing. The site should feel like it was made by someone with taste and technical ability — a creative developer, not a business-card site. Confident without being loud. Every detail earned.

## References

- **vivre.agency** — dark, high contrast, restrained copy, ample negative space, cinematic imagery. "Presence, carefully composed." The gold standard for the mood.
- **The existing index page** — the full-screen video hero with typing name and circle frame. This feel is right and should anchor the aesthetic system across all pages.
- **brittanychiang.com** — developer-portfolio sharpness: strong type hierarchy, dark theme, intentional color accent.

## Anti-references

- Startup landing page energy: big CTAs, hero metrics, testimonials, "what I can do for you" framing.
- Generic resume sites: timeline-heavy, bullet-point dense, LinkedIn-shaped.
- Overly experimental art portfolios: interface as puzzle, motion for motion's sake, navigation that requires effort.
- Anything that could belong to anyone. This site should only work for Nikhil.

## Design Principles

1. **Life over resume.** Show the whole person — the athlete, the builder, the creative — not a filtered-for-hiring version. Breadth is a feature, not a distraction.
2. **Presence, not pitch.** No selling. No CTAs. Let the work and the story speak. The reader reaches out when they're ready.
3. **Craft as credential.** The way the site is built IS part of the statement. Technical ability shows through what the site does and how it feels, not just what it says.
4. **Cinematic restraint.** Strong visual moments separated by real breathing room. One thing at a time. Density is the enemy.
5. **Earned confidence.** Every element justifies its existence. Nothing decorative that doesn't serve the story.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all typing animations, scroll effects, and transitions should have a reduced-motion fallback. No critical information conveyed by color alone.
